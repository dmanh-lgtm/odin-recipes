Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian oven-baked dish

1. The Traditional Italian (Bolognese) Method This version focuses on rich, slow-cooked flavors and uses a creamy white sauce instead of ricotta. The Sauce (Ragù): A hearty meat sauce made by sautéing a soffritto (onion, carrot, and celery) with ground beef and pork. It is simmered with tomatoes and often a splash of red wine for several hours to deepen the flavor. The Cream (Béchamel): A smooth, white sauce made from a roux of butter and flour whisked with milk and a pinch of nutmeg. The Assembly: Thin sheets of egg pasta are layered with the meat ragù, the creamy béchamel, and a generous dusting of Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese. It is baked until the top is golden and bubbling.

2. The Classic American (Ricotta) Method This popular version is known for its gooey, cheesy texture and the use of ricotta cheese as a central layer. The Meat Layer: A savory mixture of ground beef and often Italian sausage, seasoned with garlic, basil, and oregano in a thick tomato-based marinara sauce. The Cheese Filling: A blend of ricotta cheese, beaten egg (to act as a binder), Parmesan, and fresh parsley. The Assembly: Wide, rippled lasagna noodles are layered with the meat sauce, the ricotta mixture, and a heavy topping of shredded mozzarella cheese. It is baked covered with foil to keep it moist, then uncovered at the end to brown the cheese.

Ingredients

  • Meat: Ground beef and ground pork (or pancetta).
  • Vegetables (Soffritto): Onion, carrot, and celery.
  • Liquids: Crushed tomatoes (or passata), tomato paste, and a splash of red wine.
  • Seasoning: Garlic, bay leaves, salt, pepper, and sometimes fresh herbs like rosemary or thyme.

Steps

  1. Make Sauces: Simmer a meat sauce and prepare a ricotta mixture or béchamel
  2. Prep Pasta: Boil noodles until al dente and drain
  3. Layer: In a baking dish, alternate sauce, noodles, and cheese until full.
  4. Bake & Rest: Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 45 minutes; let sit for 15 minutes before serving to set the layers.
